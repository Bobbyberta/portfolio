Crafting a Sci-Fi Storytelling Experience: A UX Journey

Interactive Design August 2025 8 min read

Artificial intelligence has become a significant part of my recent work, and I've found myself fascinated by the conversational nature of our interactions. This sparked an idea: what if I could capture this experience within a narrative? I envisioned a story where the AI wasn't just a tool but an active participant, a character that converses with you, shaping the unfolding story. This concept became the foundation for my latest project—a sci-fi mystery exploring the subversion of information and the quest for hidden truths.

The Concept: AI as Character

The story is set in a future where a critical piece of history has been erased, and a small group of individuals must uncover it. Their primary method? AI hacking techniques, inspired by real-world methods documented before 2025. I wanted to create a narrative that not only delved into the technical aspects of AI but also emphasized the human element. The journey to truth requires more than just hacking the machine; it requires connecting with people, asking the right questions, and piecing together the puzzle from both human and artificial sources.

This led to the creation of four distinct characters, each playing a vital role in unraveling the mystery. My initial ambition was to build a branching narrative, allowing the player to make choices that would alter the story's direction. While I developed this in Twine, the complexity of translating it to JSON for the web app proved to be a significant hurdle. For now, the story follows a more linear path, but the branching logic remains a potential future enhancement.

Complete Figma design system showing the home screen, inbox navigation, and chat interface that would define the entire user experience

Collaborative Writing with AI

To bring this world to life, I collaborated with Gemini, using it as a writing partner to map out the story, flesh out the script, and even critique and refine various sections. Once the narrative began to take shape, I moved to Figma to sketch out a minimalist UI, focusing on a design that would allow for rapid prototyping. From there, I used Cursor to translate my designs into a functional web app, building the game engine to deliver the narrative in a compelling, chat-based format.

Technical Challenges: From Twine to Web

One of the most significant technical challenges was converting the 9,900-word story from Twine's format into a structured JSON file that the web app could understand. With the help of Cursor, I wrote Python scripts to transform the narrative into approximately 500 individual chat messages. The non-linear way Twine stores story elements required several iterations to perfect the numbering system and ensure a seamless flow.

The original Twine story structure showing the complex branching narrative that needed to be converted to a linear chat format

My initial plan was to develop this project in Unity to create a dedicated app experience. However, the complexity of setting up the UI in Unity, compared to the streamlined process of web development, led me to pivot. The web app allowed for quicker testing and iteration of the narrative, and the JSON structure I created can still be adapted for the Unity version in the future.

Design Philosophy: Minimalism Meets Functionality

The design approach centered on creating a chat-based interface that would feel familiar to users while maintaining the immersive quality of the sci-fi setting. I focused on minimalist aesthetics that wouldn't distract from the narrative while ensuring the interface could handle the complexity of multiple character conversations and branching dialogue paths.

The UI needed to support rapid prototyping and easy content updates, which influenced my choice of web technologies over more complex game engines. This decision proved valuable during the iterative process of refining the story flow and character interactions.

Deployment and Iteration

Hosting the project on GitHub Pages was a natural choice. It's a free, straightforward solution for projects without a back-end, and I had already used it to host the original Twine prototype. This approach has made it easy to share the experience with friends and gather valuable feedback.

The deployment process also allowed for rapid iteration. As I received feedback on the narrative flow and character development, I could quickly implement changes and redeploy, making the development cycle much more agile than traditional game development approaches.

Key Insights and Learnings

  • AI can serve as both subject matter and creative collaborator in narrative design
  • Web technologies offer significant advantages for narrative prototyping and iteration
  • Converting between narrative tools requires careful planning of data structure
  • Minimalist UI design allows the story to take center stage
  • Rapid deployment enables continuous feedback and improvement cycles

Looking Forward

This project has been a rewarding journey through narrative design, UI development, and the overall user experience. It's a testament to the power of AI as a creative partner and the importance of agile development in bringing a complex vision to life. I'm excited to continue refining the story and exploring new ways to blend technology and storytelling.

The experience has opened up new possibilities for interactive narrative design, particularly in how AI can be integrated not just as a tool but as a character within the story itself. As AI technology continues to evolve, the opportunities for creating immersive, conversational experiences will only expand.
